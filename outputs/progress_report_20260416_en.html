Architecture Exploration Notes

Most of the new architecture work has focused on making HEN more integrated, more efficient, and more compatible with incremental training. These ideas are engineering successes, but they have not yet produced the strongest accuracy results.

Modular Shared-Trunk HEN

  • The training flow for a shared trunk plus detachable branches has been validated.
  • Local branch fine-tuning does not significantly damage parallel branches; prepared_food fine-tuning improved global leaf accuracy from 94.67% to 94.81%.
  • The main drawback is that overall accuracy still stays below the older HEN line.

Coarse-to-Fine / Residual HEN

  • The direction of a light top router plus stronger downstream experts worked at the top level: the top router reached 99.33%.
  • The best mid stage reached 97.41%, but the full leaf model only reached 92.37%.
  • This suggests routing is already strong, while leaf-level capacity or training strategy is still insufficient.

Food Branch Specialization

  • Several targeted ideas were tested for the food branch: higher-resolution mid input, conservative crop policy, shared-feature correction, and attention crop.
  • None of these raised food mid accuracy beyond 95.78%.
  • The attention crop mechanism was integrated successfully, but it did not deliver a measurable gain.

Lightweight Search

  • MobileNetV3 and ShuffleNet backbones have been wired into the flat, joint HEN, and modular HEN code paths.
  • At the moment, the 1M-parameter scale is still not enough to hold the 95% line.
  • The next promising range is likely the 3M to 5M scale, for example MobileNetV3-Large or comparable backbones.

Current Assessment

If the goal is to maximize accuracy, the most stable story remains: classic HEN works, but the best flat baseline is still stronger. If the goal is to build a more engineering-friendly, incrementally trainable hierarchical model, modular HEN and joint HEN already provide credible prototypes, but they have not yet achieved the best trade-off between accuracy and efficiency.

Most stable conclusion Hierarchical structure is useful, but the best HEN is still about 1.04 percentage points below the best flat baseline.
Most solid engineering result Modular incremental training has been validated and supports a shared trunk with localized branch updates.
Most likely next step Push lightweight search into the 3M to 5M range, or return to the classic HEN line with stronger leaf experts.
